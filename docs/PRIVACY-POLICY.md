## Introduction

CredentialDOMD, Inc. ("CredentialDOMD," "we," "us," or "our") operates a subscription software platform that helps licensed physicians organize, maintain, and manage their professional credentials. We take the privacy of your professional information seriously.

This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect from you, why we collect it, how we protect it, and what rights you have over it. Please read it carefully. By creating an account or using our platform, you agree to the practices described here.

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to all information collected through:

- The CredentialDOMD web application and any associated mobile applications

## 2. Who We Are and What We Do

CredentialDOMD is a SaaS platform for individual physicians. We are not a healthcare provider, a covered entity under HIPAA, or a credentialing verification organization (CVO). We act as a **personal credential management tool** -- a secure, organized repository that belongs to you and exists for your benefit.

Because we handle sensitive professional and identity information belonging to licensed medical professionals, we voluntarily apply HIPAA-adjacent data handling principles and enterprise-grade security practices even where not legally mandated.

## 6. How We Protect Your Information

Security is not optional for a platform holding sensitive professional records. We have implemented the following controls:

### 6.1 Encryption

- **In transit:** All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. HTTPS is enforced across all endpoints.
- **At rest:** All data stored in our Supabase-managed PostgreSQL database and file storage is encrypted at rest using AES-256. This includes all uploaded documents (license certificates, malpractice policies, CME certificates, etc.).

### 6.2 Access Control

- **Row-level security (RLS):** Our database enforces row-level security policies at the database engine level. This means that even if an application-layer bug existed, authenticated users are cryptographically prevented from querying another user's records. Each user can only ever access their own data.
- **Authentication:** Passwords are hashed using bcrypt. We support multi-factor authentication (MFA) and strongly recommend it.
- **Internal access:** CredentialDOMD employees and contractors with access to production systems are limited to those with a specific operational need. Access is logged and audited.

### 6.3 Infrastructure

- Our infrastructure is hosted on Supabase (which runs on AWS) and Vercel/Netlify, both of which maintain SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications.
- We apply least-privilege principles to all API keys, service accounts, and database roles.
- Production secrets are stored in environment-variable vaults -- never in source code.

### 6.4 Ongoing Security Practices

- Dependency updates and vulnerability scanning are performed on a regular cadence.
- We conduct periodic access reviews to revoke unused credentials and permissions.
- We maintain an incident response plan (see Section 10).

No system is perfectly impenetrable. We commit to applying industry-standard controls proportionate to the sensitivity of the data we hold and to acting promptly if something goes wrong.

## 7. Your Rights and Choices

You have meaningful control over your data. The following rights apply regardless of your jurisdiction:

### 7.1 Access

You can view all data associated with your account at any time by logging into the platform. A summary of all stored records is available in your account settings.

### 7.2 Correction

You can edit or correct any credential record, document, or account detail directly within the platform at any time.

### 7.3 Export

You have the right to export all of your data in a portable format. We provide:

- **Structured export:** Download all credential records as a CSV or JSON file from the Settings > Data Export page.
- **Document export:** Download all uploaded files (certificates, policy documents, etc.) as a ZIP archive.

Export requests are fulfilled immediately within the platform, with no need to contact support.

### 7.4 Deletion

You can permanently delete your account -- and all associated data -- at any time from Settings > Account > Delete Account. Deletion is **permanent and irreversible.** We will:

- Remove all credential records, uploaded documents, and account metadata from our active database within **72 hours.**
- Remove all data from our backups within **30 days** of the deletion request (backup retention cycle).

Some minimal data may be retained longer if required by law (for example, billing records for tax purposes, retained for up to 7 years as required by U.S. tax law).

### 7.5 Restriction and Objection

If you believe we are processing your data in a way you did not authorize, contact us at [privacy@credentialdomd.com](mailto:privacy@credentialdomd.com) and we will investigate and respond within 10 business days.

## 10. Security Breach Notification

In the event we discover or reasonably suspect a data breach that may have exposed your personal or credential data, we commit to:

1. **Contain the breach** as immediately as possible, including revoking compromised credentials and isolating affected systems.
2. **Notify affected users by email within 72 hours** of confirming a breach that presents material risk of harm -- or sooner if technically feasible.
3. **Notify applicable regulatory authorities** (including state attorneys general where required by state breach notification law) within legally required timeframes.
4. **Publish a public incident report** on our website within 30 days of confirming a material breach, describing what happened, what data was involved, what we did, and what you should do.

Our breach notification email will always come from **security@credentialdomd.com**. It will never ask you to click a link to "re-enter your password." If you receive a suspicious email claiming to be a CredentialDOMD breach notice, contact us directly to verify.
